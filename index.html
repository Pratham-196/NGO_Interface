What is an NGO?

An NGO is an independent, non-profit group formed by people who come together to work for social, cultural, environmental, or humanitarian causes.

Unlike businesses, NGOs don't aim to make profits; instead, they focus on solving problems in society and helping people or nature.

Featured NGOs

WWF (World Wide Fund for Nature)

WWF works to sustain the natural world for the benefit of people and wildlife, collaborating with partners from local to global levels in nearly 100 countries.

MSF (Doctors Without Borders) provides emergency medical care to people affected by conflicts, epidemics, disasters, and exclusion from healthcare, working with communities in over 70 countries worldwide.

Greenpeace

Greenpeace works to protect the environment and promote peace by addressing global issues such as climate change, deforestation, overfishing, and pollution, campaigning in over 55 countries through nonviolent action and advocacy.
